Computer Networking

Software Defined Future Access Network Architecture for MAC Layer Scheduling

Future Access Networks

We proposed a Software-Defined Future Access Network architecture that can help to replace the hardware-based bandwidth distribution component into software- based bandwidth distribution component. The bandwidth distribution component permits pluggable bandwidth distribution algorithms among multiple ONUs adaptive to traffic scenarios and network operational conditions. In our proposed SDN architecture, the controller helps to control the flow of data traffic by segregating the traffic is divided into two types; one for high priority (HP) data traffic and other for low priority (LP) data traffic.

Brain Tumor Radiogenomic Classification using Transfer Learning

Deep Learning

Brain tumor classification is a crucial aspect of medical applications that rely on computer-aided diagnosis. Misdiagnosing the type of brain tumor can hinder the patient's response to treatment and reduce their chances of survival. This paper presents a novel approach to classifying brain cancers in MRI scans using traditional CNN techniques and transfer learning methods. The proposed method can effectively classify the type of tumor and assist doctors in treating the patient according to their diagnosis. To achieve this, we employed transfer learning techniques using traditional CNN models such as MobileNetV2, Inception ResNet50, and VGG16 networks.
